## Liberty Church Decorum
1. It shall be the duty of the church to meet monthly in conference when each member shall attend without some lawful excuse.
2. In absence of the pastor, the church shall choose a moderator who's duty it shall be to give an opportunity for the reception of members.
3. Take up reference the oldest first.
4. It is the duty of each member to vote in all cases and majority shall rule except touching fellowship.
5. No motion made shall claim the attention of the moderator without a second.
6. Every motion made and seconded shall come under the observation of the church except it be withdrawn by the member who made it.
7. Every member who speaks in debate shall rise from his seat and address the moderator and shall not be interrupted while speaking unless he departs from the subject.
8. No member shall speak more than three times on the same subject without leave of the church.
9. No member shall have the liberty of laughing or whispering in the time of public speaking nor cast any reflection on the speaker, he shall strictly adhere to the subject.
10. No member shall absent himself from the conference without leave of the moderator.
11. The moderator shall have the same privilege to speak as another member provided the chair be filled and he shall have no vote except the conference be equally divided.
12. If any member shall fail to attend church meetings without having some lawful and reasonable excuse shall be under question sincere of the church.
13. It shall be the duty of the deacons while taking the Lord's Supper to notice whether any of the members are mixing with the congregation, and if any, the deacons should report them to the next conference.
14. It shall be the duty of the moderator to see that the rules are observed.
15. Any amendments may be made to these rules any time the church may think proper.

## ARTICLES OF FAITH
1. We believe there is one true and living God, and that the Godhead consists of three Divine Persons, The Father, and The Son, and The Holy Spirit. (Deu. 4:39, Matt. 28:19, Mark 12:32 and I John 5:7).
2. We believe Jesus Christ to be The Son of God. (Matt. 3:16-17 and Luke 1:35).
3. We believe that all scripture is given by inspiration of God, and that the ministers of Christ should teach all the scripture recorded in the 1611 King James Version. However, we believe that the New Testament contains the perfect rule of order, discipline and practice for the Church of Christ. (Acts 20:26-28, II Cor. 3:6, Heb. 7:12 and II Tim. 3:16-17).
4. We believe that by one man - Adam - sin entered into the world, and by that sin death passed upon all men, and that "by nature" the human race is alienated from God, and are without strength, desire or will to come to Christ for life. (John 6:44, Rom. 3:9-18, Eph. 2:1-3 and Titus 3:3-7).
5. We believe that by the degree of God, and for the manifestation of His glorious grace, He chose an innumerable host of mankind, and predestinated them to be conformed to the image of His Son Jesus Christ. Those He chose are particular and unchangeably designed, so that they cannot either increase or diminish. (Rom. 8:29-30, Rom. 9:10-16, Eph. 1:3-6 and Rev. 7:9)
6. We believe that those of mankind thus chosen and fore-ordained unto eternal life, were redeemed by Christ Jesus, and are effectually called by the direct work of the Holy Spirit, and that by irresistible grace. We, thus reject the doctrines of water baptism regeneration and gospel regeneration by the preached word. (Ps. 65:4, John 1:10-13, John 3:5-8, John 6:63, John 10:27-30, Eph. 2:1, Gal. 4:6, Jude 1:1 and I Peter 1:18-19).
7. We believe in the preservation of the saints; that they are "preserved" in Jesus Christ, and can never finally fall away from the love of God and the redemption that is by Jesus Christ. (John 5:24, Rom. 8:33-39, Phil. 1:6 and Jude 1:1).
8. We believe that baptism and the Lord's Supper are Ordinances of Christ, and baptism by immersion in water is the only scriptural mode, and that unleavened bread and wine are the only proper emblems for the Lord's Supper, and that the authority to administer these ordinances is invested in God's ministers who have come under the imposition of hands by a presbytery. (Matt. 3:17-27, Matt. 26:26-29, John 3:23 and I Cor. 11:23-26).
9. We believe that Jesus Christ taught one of the greatest examples of humility when He laid aside His garments, and washed the apostles feet. We believe that the Church of Christ should practice this example in all ages. (John 13:4-17).
10. We believe that there shall be a resurrection of the dead, both of the just and unjust, and that the bodies of the elect shall inherit the Kingdom prepared for them "from the foundation of the world", and that the bodies of the wicked "shall go away into everlasting punishment". (Dan. 12:2, Matt. 25:34-46, Acts 24:15, I Cor. 15:35-55 and Rev. 20:11-15).
11. We deny that God decreed sin, and that He is not in any sense the author thereof. We believe the scriptures teach a parental law, and that the saints of God that obey the scriptures enjoy blessings in this life which cannot be enjoyed otherwise. (Isa. 1:19-20, Luke 12:24, I Cor. 3:8, James 1:13-15 and James 1:25).
12. We believe that the Churches of Christ should withdraw from every member that walks disorderly; that walks in any sin named in Gal. 5:19-21 or Eph. 5:5. (Rom. 16:17-18, I Cor. 6:10 and II Thes. 3:6).
13. We believe in the separation of Church and State, and that each local church is an organic member of the visible Kingdom of Jesus Christ, and that such a church is an independent body, having Christ as her only Head and Lawgiver. (Matt. 23:21, John 18:36, Eph. 5:23, Eph. 4:15-16 and Col. 1:18).

## CHURCH COVENANT
### LIBERTY PRIMITIVE BAPTIST CHURCH, SMITHVILLE, GEORGIA

Having been made, as we believe, by the Spirit of God, to know Jesus Christ in the free pardon of our sins and to love God with pure hearts fervently, and on the profession of our faith, having been baptized in the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit, we do now in the presences of God and this assembly, most solemnly enter covenant with one another as one body in Christ.

We promise, therefore, by the assistance of the Holy Spirit, to walk together in brotherly love; to strive for the advancement of this church in knowledge, holiness, and comfort; to promote its prosperity and spirituality; to sustain its worship, ordinances, discipline, and doctrine; to be faithful in church attendance; to contribute cheerfully and regularly as the Lord has prospered us to the support of the ministry, the expenses of the church, and the relief of the poor.

We also agree to maintain family and secret devotions; to religiously educate children; to be just in our dealings, faithful in our engagements, and exemplary in our conduct; to avoid whispering, backbiting, gossip, excessive anger, and speaking evil one of another, and to live at each others feet, in love, preferring others before ourselves.

We further engage to watch over one another in brotherly love; to pray for one another; to be slow to take offense and ready to forgive those who may offend us. We agree to labor for reconciliation by reproving those who err, in the most tender and affectionate manner, according to the directions given by our Lord in the eighteenth chapter of Matthew.

We also covenant to keep ourselves unspotted from the world, living separate lives, in obedience to the authority of God's word and submission to the Lordship of Jesus Christ, dedicated to the honor and glory of God.

We commit ourselves together to labor for the peace and unity of this body, to be zealous to promote the Kingdom of God, to assist, as we are able, those of like precious faith in their attempts to advance the gospel of grace even beyond our local body, and to earnestly contend for the faith once delivered to the saints.

Under God, we freely make this covenant, in the spirit of Nehemiah chapter ten, and agree "not to forsake the house of our God." This covenant shall not be employed as a law, but as a voluntary agreement to be used as an incentive to promote individual faithfulness, and as a standard by which to measure one's own personal godliness.
