### Spectrum Telecom Infrastructure

* Bill Johnson and Will Taff met with Spectrum's regional technical lead and his senior field technician. Key findings and outcomes:
  * The building has **dedicated fiber optic cable** feeding directly into the network closet with a **dedicated node and network interface** — shared with no other building in the area. The Spectrum engineer noted this is highly unusual; even hospitals and government buildings typically do not have this configuration.
  * Original termination blocks on each floor were improperly wired, likely by subcontractors when Spectrum first took over the property. This is the probable cause of recurring unit-level service complaints.
  * All termination blocks have now been rewired by Spectrum's senior technician.
  * Current infrastructure supports up to **1 Gbps** service today; the existing coaxial distribution plant can support **10 Gbps** in the future without rewiring any individual units.
  * **Recommendation:** Bill and Will recommend renegotiating the bulk contract. Key asks should include: a 1 Gbps minimum speed guarantee with automatic future increases, Xumo streaming boxes, and managed Wi-Fi routers to eliminate coverage gaps in units. Will to circulate a written recap of the full recommendation list to the board. *(Bill/Will)*
