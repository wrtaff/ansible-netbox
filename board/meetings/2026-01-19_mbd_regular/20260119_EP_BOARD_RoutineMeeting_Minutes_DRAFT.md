While general homeowner satisfaction with Spectrum remains stable, the Board is concerned with the building’s coaxial infrastructure (specifically when juxtaposed against several specific unit technical complaints) that could eventually put the property at a competitive disadvantage compared to newer developments with fiber. The immediate priority is to assess the building's physical infrastructure (reference ongoing site visit coordination with Spectrum Engineering). As not to encumber the minutes, the board agreed to table until the engineering assessment is complete*.*

# **New Business** 

* **Cart usage and return**: The Board discussed the recurring issue of carts not being returned promptly to the cart room, often being retained inside units for extended periods (e.g., during renovations).  There were so few carts available for tenant use that the desk attendants were scouring the property to find them, and TSG sent an email to all homeowners.  Currently, all but one cart have been accounted for. While the installation of electronic trackers has been used in the past, the Board decided to defer for now. Instead, the Board agreed to leverage the building attendants, who generally know who is using the carts. The primary solution will be increased enforcement: attendants will report unreturned carts to TSG, and residents who keep carts overnight may be subject to fines. A reminder email has already been sent, and the Board hopes this, combined with potential fines, will resolve the issue without requiring technical interventions. (TSG)  
* **Trash bags & disposal**: Bill (TSG) reported that a reminder email was recently sent to all residents via CINC following an incident where improperly sealed trash containing liquids was dropped down the chute. The Board discussed ongoing issues with the use of unauthorized bags that contribute to the chute getting clogged and messes in the trash cart below. It was reiterated that only standard 13-gallon kitchen trash bags are authorized for use in the chute system. Residents are reminded that all bags must be securely tied and properly sized to ensure the system remains operational and sanitary. Bill (TSG) noted that attendants will continue to monitor the trash room for non-compliant disposal, to the point of searching non-compliant bags for identification and subsequent fines. (TSG)  
* **Gym upgrades**: The Board discussed the continued improvement of the building’s gym facilities. To leverage the enthusiasm and expertise of several residents, the Board explored the formation of an ad hoc gym committee to review existing equipment and recommend future upgrades. The goal is to ensure the facility remains safe, modern, and aligned with community needs while maintaining rigorous Board oversight on safety and liability. Further discussion on committee structure is ongoing, with significant interest already expressed by both Board members and residents.
